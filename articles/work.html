Where Filipinos Work in Bali

Filipinos in Bali • Published May 25, 2026 • 6 min read

Bali runs on tourism — and tourism runs on people who can make guests feel cared for. That is exactly why Filipino workers are in demand across the island. Here is a look at the industries where kababayans build careers, and how to find your footing.

Why Bali employers look for Filipinos

Hotels and resorts across Asia have a long-standing appreciation for Filipino staff, and Bali is no different. Employers consistently point to the same qualities: strong English communication, genuine warmth, adaptability, and a service-first mindset. In an industry built on hospitality, those traits are not "soft skills" — they are the product. Many Filipinos also arrive with formal training in hospitality, tourism, or wellness, which gives them a real edge.

The industries where kababayans thrive

1. Hotels & resorts

This is the biggest employer of all. At any given time there are hundreds of hospitality vacancies across Bali — front office, reservations, food and beverage, housekeeping supervision, guest relations, and management. International groups with a strong presence on the island include Marriott, Hilton, Grand Hyatt Bali, Alila, Hard Rock Hotel Bali, and large local hospitality groups. Experience in international cuisine, management, or guest service travels well.

2. Spa & wellness

Bali is one of the world's wellness capitals, and licensed massage therapists, spa therapists, and beauty specialists are in steady demand at resort spas and standalone wellness centers. A recognized therapy certification and a professional attitude go a long way here.

3. Music & entertainment

Filipino musicians have been the heartbeat of hotel lounges across Asia for generations. In Bali, singers, bands, and entertainment performers find work in resorts, bars, and beach clubs. Entertainment coordinators and hosts who can run a room are valued too.

4. Diving & watersports

With world-class dive sites, Bali needs certified dive instructors and divemasters. If you hold PADI/SSI instructor certifications and have logged experience, this is a niche where Filipinos do very well.

5. Hair, beauty & barbering

Skilled hairstylists, barbers, and nail technicians are sought after by salons serving both tourists and the expat community.

6. Teaching & international schools

Across Indonesia, many Filipinos work as teachers in international schools, where English fluency is essential. Bali has a growing number of international and private schools that hire qualified educators and teaching assistants.

7. Remote & online work

A newer path: Filipinos employed by companies outside Indonesia — in BPO, design, IT, virtual assistance, and content — who live in Bali and work online. This route uses a remote-worker visa rather than a local work permit (see our visa guide).

How to find a job in Bali

  • Job platforms — Jobstreet Indonesia, Indeed Indonesia, and Glassdoor regularly list Bali hospitality and spa roles.
  • Hotel career pages — large resorts post openings directly on their own websites.
  • Referrals — in hospitality, a recommendation from someone already inside is gold. This is where the community matters.
  • The BaliFILs group — kababayans regularly share legitimate job leads. Ask, and pay it forward when you can.

💼 Get the paperwork right

A real job offer in Bali should come with a proper work KITAS arranged by your employer. If a "job" expects you to work on a tourist visa, treat that as a red flag. Read our Visas & KITAS guide before you sign anything.

⚠️ Avoid recruitment scams

Never pay a "placement fee" to secure a job, and be wary of offers that seem too good to be true. Verify the employer exists, ask to speak with current staff, and confirm the job through official channels. When in doubt, ask BaliFILs community — chances are someone knows the company.

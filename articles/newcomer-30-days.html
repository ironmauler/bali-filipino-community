Your First 30 Days in Bali — A Filipino's Checklist

Filipinos in Bali•Published May 26, 2026

The first month in Bali decides whether you spend the next year stressed or settled. This checklist breaks it into four manageable weeks. Tick them off in order, ask BaliFILs group when you get stuck, and you'll be in good shape before your first Sunday Mass.

Week 1 — Land, breathe, communicate

  • Buy a local SIM the day you land. Telkomsel and XL Axiata have airport counters and reliable coverage; bring your passport for registration.
  • Install the essentials: Gojek and Grab (transport, food, deliveries), Google Maps (download offline maps), WhatsApp (everyone uses it), Wise (FX), and the Magma Indonesia app (volcano alerts).
  • Stay short-term first. Book a kos or modest guesthouse for 1–2 weeks while you scout neighbourhoods.
  • Pick a base area: Sanur (calm, family), Denpasar (working, central), Canggu (young, expat), Ubud (cultural, inland), Nusa Dua (resort, where many BaliFILs members live).
  • Cash: withdraw IDR from a major bank ATM (BCA, Mandiri, BNI) — avoid free-standing tourist ATMs.

Week 2 — A roof, wheels, paperwork

  • Long-term housing: a kos for singles is Rp 1.5–3.5M/month; a basic 1BR rental Rp 5–10M. See Renting in Bali Without Getting Scammed before signing.
  • Scooter: rent monthly first (Rp 700k–1.2M). Buy a helmet that actually fits. Remember Bali drives on the LEFT.
  • Driving licence: International Driving Permit from the Philippines is your fastest legal cover; convert to Indonesian SIM at the local Polres within your first months.
  • KITAS & Imigrasi: if you entered on a C312 work visa, you have a strict ~30-day window to convert to KITAS at your local immigration office. See A Filipino's KITAS How-To.
  • Greet your banjar: ask your landlord to introduce you to the kelian banjar. Read The Banjar — How to Be a Good Neighbour.

Week 3 — Money, health, community

  • Open a local bank account (BCA is the easiest with a KITAS). You'll need this for salary, rent, and Gojek top-ups.
  • Remittance setup: link Wise or Remitly to send money home. See Sending Money Home from Bali.
  • NPWP (tax ID): required if you're earning taxable income in Indonesia — your employer's HR can help.
  • Health insurance: get proper expat cover (Cigna, Allianz, AXA, Prudential Indonesia). See Healthcare in Bali.
  • Find your hospital: save the address and phone of the nearest reputable private hospital. Get the rabies series if you'll be near free-roaming dogs — see A Health Watch-List for Bali.
  • Join BaliFILs: post a quick hello in the BaliFILs Facebook group. The community moves fast and is generous.

Week 4 — Settle, register, look up

  • SKTT (Surat Keterangan Tempat Tinggal): local residence certificate from your district's Disdukcapil. Useful for banking and ID.
  • Register with the Migrant Workers Office (MWO) at the Philippine Embassy in Jakarta if you came as a direct hire and skipped DMW processing.
  • Sunday Mass: find your parish — see Where to Find Catholic Mass in Bali.
  • Stock the pantry: read A Taste of Home for kababayan-friendly ingredients and where to find them.
  • Show up to a BaliFILs event: real-world meet-ups are where the community actually happens. See the Events page.

🙋 The 30-day mindset

Don't try to solve everything in week one. Settle one bucket at a time. Ask early, ask often — kababayans have been through every single one of these and will happily save you a week of pain.

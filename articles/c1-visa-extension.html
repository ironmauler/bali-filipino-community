Extending Your Indonesian C1 Tourist Visa — A Kababayan's Guide

Filipinos in Bali•Published June 2026

The C1 (211A) tourist visa is the go-to visa for Filipinos who want to stay in Indonesia longer than the 30-day ASEAN visa-free window — think a full holiday, an extended visit with family, or a working trial before committing to a KITAS. It arrives as a 60-day permit, and it can be extended twice, 60 days each time, up to a total 180 days without leaving the country. Here is how the extension actually works.

🇵🇭 First: do you need a C1 at all?

Filipinos are ASEAN citizens and can enter Indonesia visa-free for up to 30 days for tourism/social visits. The C1 is only needed if you want to stay longer than that on a tourist basis. If you plan to work, live long-term, or run a business — that's a KITAS conversation. See our Visas & KITAS overview and Filipino KITAS how-to.

The five-step extension flow

1. Start online, ~14 days before expiry

Log in to the official Indonesian immigration eVisa portal at evisa.imigrasi.go.id, choose the option to extend your stay permit for the C1 category, and upload the required documents (see checklist below). Do not start too early or wait until after expiry — most immigration offices strictly enforce the roughly 14-day window before expiry.

2. Book & attend biometrics at the local immigration office

Even though you start online, the system will schedule you an in-person appointment at your local kantor imigrasi for a photo and fingerprints and a short document check. In Bali, that's the immigration office in your address's jurisdiction — Denpasar covers most of the south and centre; Singaraja covers the north.

3. Wait for approval

After biometrics, immigration processes the extension. Many approvals come through within a few working days, but leave slack around public holidays and peak periods. If yours is stuck on "Waiting Officer Confirmation" for more than a week, follow up.

4. Receive your new permit — digital, no sticker

Once approved you receive an ITK (Izin Tinggal Kunjungan) as a digital document. There is no new sticker glued into your passport; the electronic ITK IS your legal proof of the extended stay. Save it to your phone, email a copy to yourself, and keep a printed copy with your passport.

5. Repeat once more if needed

The same process can be repeated one more time for another 60 days — bringing your total legal stay to 180 days. After that you must exit Indonesia.

C1 extension — the numbers

  • Base stay on arrival60 days
  • Extensions permitted2 × 60 days
  • Maximum legal stay180 days total
  • Start extension~14 days before expiry
  • Where to startevisa.imigrasi.go.id
  • BiometricsRequired in person
  • Overstay fineIDR 1,000,000 per day

What you need to have ready

  • Passport (valid at least 6+ months) and the passport details page
  • Current C1 visa info and entry stamp
  • Immigration-spec passport photo
  • Proof of onward travel (a flight booking out of Indonesia)
  • Sponsor/guarantor letter if your visa required one on arrival
  • Payment for the government fee (PNBP) — pay online through the portal

Fees

Immigration charges a government fee (PNBP) for each extension, paid through the eVisa portal. Amounts change from time to time — check the current figure when you apply. If you use a visa agent, their service fee is on top of the government fee. For simple, clean extensions many kababayans do it themselves; agents earn their keep on trickier cases (last-minute, jurisdictional issues, tricky documents).

⚠️ Overstay is expensive fast

The fine is IDR 1,000,000 per day of overstay — roughly PHP 3,600 a day. Overstay beyond 60 days can lead to detention, deportation, and re-entry bans. Start the extension early. If life happens and you're going to be late, contact immigration before your expiry date rather than after.

Smart moves for kababayans

  • Set two reminders — one 30 days before expiry (start prep), one 14 days before (submit).
  • Keep digital copies of everything — passport pages, current permit, receipts, ITK.
  • Ask the BaliFILs group before choosing a visa agent — kababayans regularly share who to trust and who to avoid.
  • Don't book a tight departure around your extension — always leave a few days of buffer between your ITK end date and your flight out.
  • Think ahead of 180 days. If Bali is becoming home, the C1 is not the long-term answer — start the KITAS conversation before you hit day 100.

When you should stop extending and think KITAS

Tourist visas are for tourism. If you find yourself extending twice because you're actually working, running a business, or settling in — the honest answer is a KITAS, not another C1. Working on the wrong visa is a fine, detention, deportation and re-entry-ban risk. See A Filipino's KITAS How-To for the legit route.

From Bali to Borneo — A Filipino's Inter-Island Travel Guide

Filipinos in Bali•Published May 26, 2026

Indonesia is the largest archipelago on earth — over 17,000 islands stretching 5,000 km. From Bali, the rest of the country is closer than you think. Here is how kababayans actually get around — to neighbouring islands and to the far corners of the country.

The three main ways to move

  • Domestic flights — fastest, often cheap, the standard for long distances.
  • Fast boats & ferries — short hops to nearby islands.
  • Pelni ships — the iconic, multi-day state-run passenger ships that connect the archipelago end to end. Slow and cheap.

Domestic airlines you'll use

The Indonesian aviation market is dense and competitive. The carriers most kababayans rely on:

  • Garuda Indonesia — full-service flag carrier; punctual, generous baggage.
  • Citilink — Garuda's low-cost arm.
  • Lion Air, Batik Air, Super Air Jet, Wings Air — the Lion Air Group fleet; widest network, mixed reputation, very cheap.
  • AirAsia Indonesia — low-cost; good promos.

Book on the airline's own site or via Traveloka / Tiket.com. Domestic flights typically allow 7–20 kg checked baggage depending on fare class.

From Bali (DPS) — popular destinations

Java

  • Jakarta (CGK) — ~1h 50m flight. Daily, every airline. From Rp 700,000 one-way on a promo.
  • Yogyakarta (YIA/JOG) — ~1h 20m. For Borobudur, Prambanan, Javanese culture.
  • Surabaya (SUB) — ~50m. East Java hub; gateway to Bromo.
  • Semarang, Solo, Malang — well connected.

Lombok & Nusa Tenggara

  • Lombok (LOP) — ~30m flight, or 2-hour fast boat from Padangbai or Serangan. The Gili Islands are reached via Lombok.
  • Labuan Bajo (LBJ) — gateway to Komodo. ~1h flight. Book early in peak.
  • Kupang (KOE) — capital of NTT province.

Sulawesi

  • Makassar (UPG) — ~1h 40m. South Sulawesi hub; gateway to Tana Toraja.
  • Manado (MDC) — ~2h 30m via connection. North Sulawesi; Bunaken diving.

Kalimantan (Indonesian Borneo)

  • Balikpapan (BPN) — East Kalimantan, oil & gas hub.
  • Banjarmasin (BDJ) — South Kalimantan.
  • Pontianak (PNK) — West Kalimantan, near the Sarawak border.
  • Travel via a Jakarta or Surabaya connection if no direct flight is available.

Sumatra

  • Medan (KNO) — Lake Toba access.
  • Padang (PDG), Pekanbaru (PKU) — usually via Jakarta.

Maluku & Papua

  • Ambon (AMQ), Sorong (SOQ) (Raja Ampat gateway), Jayapura (DJJ) — typically via Jakarta or Makassar.

Ferries & fast boats

  • Bali → Lombok: fast boats from Padangbai (~2h) and Serangan; slow public ferry from Padangbai (~4–5h, very cheap).
  • Bali → Nusa Penida / Lembongan / Ceningan: fast boats from Sanur (~30–45m).
  • Bali → Java (Banyuwangi): ferries cross the Bali Strait from Gilimanuk to Ketapang (~1h).
  • Bali → Komodo: 3–4 day liveaboard tours are popular.

Pelni — the great ships

PT Pelni operates a fleet of large passenger ships that connect dozens of Indonesian ports. From Bali (Benoa), Pelni ships run on long, looping routes that touch Java, Sulawesi, Kalimantan, NTT, Maluku, and beyond. Tickets are cheap; journeys take days. The atmosphere is part-cruise, part-village. A genuinely unforgettable way to see Indonesia. Book through pelni.co.id or at the Benoa port office.

Smart kababayan moves

  • Always carry your KITAS on domestic travel — domestic airline check-in may ask.
  • Book early for Eid (Idul Fitri / Lebaran), Christmas, New Year, and school holidays — fares triple.
  • Travel light — domestic baggage limits are tight; weighed checked + carry-on.
  • Allow time at small airports — they're informal; arrive 90 minutes ahead.
  • Layovers are not seamless — sometimes you re-check bags and pass security again.

🇲🇾 To Malaysia or East Malaysia

Kuching, Kota Kinabalu, and other East Malaysian destinations (also on Borneo) are reachable as international flights from Jakarta or Singapore. Land crossings from Indonesian to Malaysian Borneo exist (Entikong-Tebedu in West Kalimantan) but require visas and good information.

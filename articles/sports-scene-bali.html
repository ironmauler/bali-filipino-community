The Filipino Sports Scene in Bali

Filipinos in Bali•Published May 26, 2026

For Filipinos, sport is a social language. Sneakers + a ball + a court = friends within an hour. Bali has the spaces and the schedule to keep that tradition going — and kababayans here have built informal leagues, pickup nights, and running groups that anyone can plug into. Here is the scene.

🏀 Basketball

The default kababayan sport. Filipinos in Indonesia have formed sports teams across several cities, and Bali is no exception. Pickup games and friendly tournaments happen at:

  • Community courts in residential neighbourhoods — especially around Denpasar, Nusa Dua and Sanur.
  • School and university courts that open to the public on weekends.
  • Resort and hotel staff courts — Filipino hospitality workers often have access.

The BaliFILs group is the fastest way to find a regular game; post a message and you'll get invited to a chat in hours.

🏐 Volleyball

The other Filipino default. Beach volleyball happens informally on Sanur and Kuta beaches; indoor volleyball happens at sports halls and through workplace teams. Mixed teams are common; everyone is welcome.

🏃 Running

Bali has an active running community across nationalities, and kababayans plug in easily. Popular routes:

  • Sanur beachfront — flat 5–10 km path, sunrise crowd.
  • Mertasari to Padang Galak — extended coastal route.
  • Canggu beach paths — surfers and runners share the dawn light.
  • Bali Hash House Harriers — international running/walking social club with weekly events.

Big races happen periodically — Bali Marathon, charity 5K/10Ks, trail runs in the highlands. Sign up early; they fill.

🏸 Badminton

Indonesia is a global badminton power, and that means courts are everywhere and good. Many residential complexes, schools, and sports centres have indoor courts you can rent by the hour. Kababayan players form casual leagues; ask in BaliFILs group for nearby courts and regular sessions.

🏊 Swimming, surfing & ocean sports

Bali is one of the world's surfing capitals. Filipino surfers find their feet on the friendlier breaks (Kuta, Berawa) before progressing. For lap swimming, hotel and gym pools welcome day passes. For freediving and scuba, see our note on dive instructors in Where Filipinos Work in Bali.

⚽ Football / soccer & futsal

Futsal halls exist across Denpasar and the south. Mixed-nationality teams compete in weekend leagues. If you played at home, you'll find a game here.

🧘 Yoga, fitness, martial arts

Bali — especially Ubud and Canggu — is one of the world's yoga capitals. Studios cater to every style and budget. Filipino martial-arts traditions (eskrima/arnis) also have small communities in Bali; the Bali Sports Foundation has historically promoted Filipino martial arts on the island.

How to find your game

  1. Post in BaliFILs Facebook group: "Looking for a pickup basketball game in Sanur — anyone?" — you'll get DMs the same day.
  2. Ask at your workplace: hotel and resort staff often have established teams.
  3. Visit a public court at 5 PM — that's when most pickup games start.
  4. Check Facebook events for one-off tournaments and BaliFILs-organised sports days.

🏆 Idea: BaliFILs Sports Day

Several other Filipino communities in Indonesia run annual sports tournaments — a one-day basketball + volleyball + family games event open to all kababayans on the island. If you'd like to help organise something like this for BaliFILs, email balifilipinos@gmail.com. Volunteers, sponsors, and venue leads always welcome.

📋 Help us build the team list

If you run or play for a Filipino sports team in Bali — basketball, volleyball, futsal, badminton, anything — send the name, contact, schedule, and area to balifilipinos@gmail.com and we'll add it to a public team directory here.

Filipino-Owned Businesses in Bali

Filipinos in Bali•A living, community-sourced directory · Updated May 2026

When kababayans support kababayans, the whole community gets stronger. This page is BaliFILs' growing directory of Filipino-owned businesses in Bali — restaurants, salons, spas, dive shops, design studios, food sellers, and more. It is built entirely from member nominations and refreshed regularly. If you run a business or know one to add, scroll to the form at the bottom.

How to use this directory

  • Every listing is nominated by a BaliFILs member and verified by an admin before going live.
  • We list only the basics — name, what they do, area, and the best way to contact them.
  • We do not rank or rate. Use your own judgement; ask for recommendations in the Facebook group.
  • If a business is no longer operating or has changed, please email us at balifilipinos@gmail.com so we can update.

📝 Nominate a business

Send us the following at balifilipinos@gmail.com with the subject line "FOB nomination":

  • Business name
  • Category (from the list above)
  • Owner's name (and confirmation they are Filipino / kababayan)
  • Area in Bali (Sanur, Canggu, Ubud, Nusa Dua, Denpasar, etc.)
  • Best contact (Facebook page, Instagram, WhatsApp, website)
  • One sentence about what they do
  • Owner's consent to be listed (a screenshot or forwarded message is enough)

We verify and add new listings monthly.

Why this matters

Most Filipino businesses in Bali start small — a home kitchen serving the neighbourhood, a one-chair barber shop in Kuta, a freelance massage therapist with a phone full of regulars. They survive on word of mouth. When BaliFILs members buy from each other, leave honest reviews, and refer friends, they keep that whole quiet ecosystem alive. It also means kababayans new to Bali always have a familiar face to turn to — for a haircut, a meal, a wedding photographer, or a ride to the airport.

🚀 For business owners

If you're running a kababayan business in Bali and want to grow:

  • Set up a Google Business profile — it's free and helps people find you.
  • Post regularly in BaliFILs Facebook group within the group's rules — don't spam.
  • Offer a small BaliFILs member discount; nothing builds loyalty faster.
  • Make sure your KITAS, business permits, and tax registration (NPWP) are in order — see our visa guide.

The directory itself
